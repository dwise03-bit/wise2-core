## TONE & LANGUAGE GUIDELINES

### Do's ✅

- Be clear and direct
- Use active voice
- Focus on benefits, not features
- Tell stories, not specs
- Be authentic and human
- Use "we" for the company, "you" for the customer
- Celebrate user wins

### Don'ts ❌

- Avoid jargon and tech speak
- Don't over-promise
- Avoid passive voice
- Don't focus on features in isolation
- Don't be corporate or stiff
- Avoid industry buzzwords ("synergy," "leverage," "paradigm")
- Never say "we're the only ones who..."

### Examples

**Feature-Focused (❌)**
"WISE Sound Labs includes multi-track editing with 50+ effects"

**Benefit-Focused (✅)**
"Mix professional audio in minutes, not hours"

## BRAND GUIDELINES: WHAT TO AVOID

### Don't:
- Use generic stock imagery
- Sound like enterprise software
- Oversell AI capabilities
- Make promises you can't keep
- Ignore accessibility
- Use outdated design trends
- Talk about features without benefit context
- Be condescending to non-experts

### Don't Compare to:
- Mention competitors by name in marketing
- Use comparative language ("we're better than X")
- Position as "the only" solution

### Do Instead:
- Acknowledge the market
- Focus on our differentiation
- Celebrate what makes us unique

## EMERGENCY COMMUNICATIONS

### Crisis Messaging Framework

**When Things Go Wrong**:
1. Acknowledge quickly
2. Take responsibility
3. Explain what we're doing
4. Commit to resolution
5. Follow up when resolved

**Example**: "We experienced downtime yesterday. This wasn't acceptable. Here's exactly what happened and what we're doing to prevent it."
