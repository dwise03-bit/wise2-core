# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary:** Founders and infrastructure operators (indie, early-stage, post-PMF).

**Context:** Shopping for production infrastructure. Tired of choosing between "broke and overwhelmed" (DIY Kubernetes) or "overcharged and locked-in" (enterprise SaaS). Work in dark environments (terminal, laptops, focused mode). Value honesty over hype.

**Job:** Validate that Wise² Core solves their infrastructure problem without wasting time on marketing fluff. Make a quick decision: "Is this for me?" → Yes/No. If yes: "Can I try it?" → Email waitlist.

## Product Purpose

Wise² Core is production-ready infrastructure: PostgreSQL, Redis, monitoring, backups, alerts—pre-configured, cost-transparent ($15–20/month), and operator-owned (no lock-in).

**Why it exists:** The market conflates "infrastructure is hard" with "infrastructure is expensive." Wise² Core splits the difference: hard-ness is solved (pre-configured), cost is solved (transparent, low), lock-in is solved (standard Docker).

**Success:** Founders see the landing page, recognize the problem it solves, give their email, and come back when they're ready to deploy.

## Brand Personality

**Three words:** Futuristic, Bold, Cinematic

**Voice:** Operators who see infrastructure as craft, not commodity. No corporate jargon. No fake urgency. Direct: "Here's what it does. Here's what it costs. Here's who uses it." Confidence without arrogance. Neon blue on dark backgrounds, like Stark's JARVIS or a hacker's terminal—beautiful, purposeful, alive.

**Tone dimensions:**
- Formal ↔ Casual: **Formal-leaning** (we're serious about infrastructure) but not corporate. No exclamation marks. Sentences are clipped, direct.
- Serious ↔ Playful: **Serious.** Infrastructure isn't play. But voice is present; copy has edge. "Stop choosing" instead of "get started."
- Expert ↔ Accessible: **Expert-for-operators.** Assume the reader knows why PostgreSQL matters. Don't over-explain. But don't gatekeep with jargon either.
- Minimal ↔ Ornate: **Minimal-to-austere.** Every word earns its place. Copy is short, active, high-signal.

**Emotional goal:** Confidence. "These people get it. They won't waste my time."

## Anti-references

**What this should NOT look like:**

- SaaS cream-and-terracotta (generic, forgettable)
- Startup hyperbole ("revolutionize," "world-class," "disrupt")
- Dark mode as an afterthought (we're committed to dark)
- Nested cards and icon-above-text grid templates
- Numbered sections with small-caps eyebrows above every heading (AI grammar)
- Blurred glassmorphic frosted-glass everywhere (dilutes the neon aesthetic)
- Stock photography of fake "diversity" stock models
- Anything that could be replaced by "generated by Claude" without anyone noticing

**Specific sites/patterns to avoid:**
- Typical SaaS landing pages (Linear, Vercel, Supabase templates). Wise² is more cinematic, less corporate-minimal.
- Crypto/web3 aesthetic (all gradients and NFT-energy). Wise² is grounded, professional.
- Generic "AI workflow tool" UI (muted, corporate, beige). We're the neon alternative.

## Design Principles

1. **One character, two perspectives.** The landing page isn't about product features—it's about the founder's journey. One story (the founder), told twice (Idea Hunter perspective, System Builder perspective). Every section serves this narrative, not a feature checklist.

2. **Neon is the commitment, not the decoration.** Neon blue isn't an accent; it's the brand's operating system. Dark background, neon everything else. Not used sparingly; used systematically. Every glow, every panel, every animation speaks neon.

3. **Floating panels as interface language.** Holographic floating panels aren't glassmorphic eye candy—they're the visual metaphor for "infrastructure is intangible but real." A panel can contain a decision, an architecture decision, a section of copy. Panels float, drift, glow. That's the interface grammar.

4. **Copy over visuals.** The voice is the main design artifact. Copy is short, direct, high-stakes. No decorative images; one hero (the comic). No unnecessary icons. Text carries the weight.

5. **Cinematic staging.** Think John Wick or Blade Runner's production design: every frame could be a poster. Dark, intentional, high-contrast, lighting that sculpts the subject. Landing page is a moment, not a scroll.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

**WCAG 2.1 AA** (minimum). Specific considerations:

- Contrast: Body text ≥4.5:1 (neon blue on dark hits this naturally). But neon is not readable for extended text; use tinted neutrals for body, neon for accent and interactive elements.
- Keyboard navigation: Form (email + button) must be fully keyboard-operable. Tab order clear.
- Reduced motion: All animations have `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)` alternatives. Fade or instant transition, never elastic.
- Color blindness: Neon blue is readable for deuteranopia and protanopia. Avoid red-green pairs as the only differentiator (use shape, text, or position too).
- Screen readers: Section hierarchy is semantic (h1, h2, h3). Form labels are associated with inputs. Alt text on the hero image (comic).
- Mobile: Touch targets ≥44px. Inputs are readable without zooming. Responsive from 320px.
