## 05 — AUDIENCE-SPECIFIC MESSAGING

**For Struggling Business Owners (Primary Audience):**

**What they care most about:**
- Cost control (can't waste money on infrastructure)
- Speed to market (need to launch fast to validate)
- Reliability (one outage could kill the business)
- Not hiring (can't afford a DevOps person)
- Simplicity (don't have time to learn DevOps)

**How to lead the message:**
Lead with cost + speed: *"Deploy production infrastructure in 10 minutes for $5/month. Stop spending time on infrastructure and start building your product."*

**Proof points that land hardest:**
- Cost comparison: "50-80% cheaper than alternatives"
- Speed: "Deploy in 10 minutes, not weeks"
- Reliability: "30+ pre-configured alerts, automated backups"
- No overhead: "No DevOps hire needed"

**Avoid:**
- Technical jargon without translation
- Anything that sounds expensive
- Complexity ("multi-region failover strategies")
- Anything that suggests you need expertise they don't have

---

**For Investors / Partners (Secondary Audience):**

**What they care most about:**
- Market opportunity (is this a real problem?)
- Business model sustainability (can you make money from this?)
- Defensibility (what's the moat?)
- Traction (who's using this?)

**How to lead the message:**
Lead with market opportunity + traction: *"$50B+ infrastructure market underserved at the SMB level. Wise² Core is the default platform for the 5M+ struggling founders building the next generation of businesses."*

**Proof points that land hardest:**
- TAM/SAM: "5M+ founders bootstrapping products"
- Traction: "500+ early-stage companies, 99.9% uptime"
- Unit economics: "Land at $5/month, expand to enterprise"
- Defensibility: "Open source foundation, community moat, proven product-market fit"

**Avoid:**
- Overpromising growth rates
- Hyperbolic market claims
- Anything that requires hand-waving

---

**For Developers / Technical Audience:**

**What they care most about:**
- Technical soundness (is this actually good?)
- Customization (can I bend it to my needs?)
- Documentation (can I actually use this?)
- Community (are other smart people using this?)

**How to lead the message:**
Lead with technical credibility: *"PostgreSQL, Redis, Docker Compose. Battle-tested stack. Fully documented. Built for developers who ship."*

**Proof points that land hardest:**
- Stack choices: "Why we chose PostgreSQL, not 10 alternatives"
- Open source: "All code available, deploy however you want"
- Documentation: "5,300+ lines covering every scenario"
- Community: "500+ developers sharing setups and improvements"

**Avoid:**
- Marketing language (sounds fake to technical people)
- Oversimplification of technical choices
- False "no-code" claims

## 07 — THINGS NOT TO SAY

**Avoid:**
- "Revolutionary," "game-changing," "cutting-edge," "world-class" — these are hollow without proof
- "Seamlessly," "effortlessly," "intuitive" — lazy adjectives that mean nothing
- "Enterprise-grade scalability" — too vague; specify what you mean
- "Trusted by thousands of businesses" — meaningless without context (who? how many? what size?)
- "Any size, any scale" — contradicts our positioning (we serve struggling founders, not Fortune 500)
- "No technical knowledge required" — false; infrastructure requires understanding (be honest)
- "We believe in..." followed by corporate philosophy — nobody cares about your beliefs
- "The future of infrastructure is..." — stop predicting; focus on what founders need today
- Comparing to "the industry" — specify the competitor or don't mention it
- "Everyone is using..." — prove it with numbers or don't say it

---

## 08 — PROOF HIERARCHY

**Strongest proof (use first in persuasion):**
- Cost comparison: "50–80% cheaper than Heroku/DigitalOcean" (compares directly to alternatives)
- Speed: "Deploy production infrastructure in 10 minutes" (specific number, specific outcome)
- Traction: "500+ early-stage companies" (social proof from the target audience)

**Medium proof (supports secondary claims):**
- Technical decisions: "PostgreSQL because [specific reason]" (shows thoughtfulness)
- Documentation depth: "5,300+ lines covering every scenario" (proof of care)
- Uptime: "99.9% uptime across deployed instances" (reliability proof)

**Weakest proof (avoid leading with these):**
- Awards or certifications (nobody cares)
- Vague testimonials ("Great platform!"—prove it with specifics)
- Our team's credentials (prove it through work, not CVs)
